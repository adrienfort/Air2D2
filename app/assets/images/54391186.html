      About This Item

      We aim to show you accurate product information. Manufacturers, suppliers and others provide what you see here, and we have not verified it.
      A loyal and dependable astromech droid well-equipped for starship repair and computer interface, even after decades of service, R2-D2 continues to be full of surprises. When the battle to take back the galaxy demands the precision and advanced technological capacities of a droid, there's no better Astromech co-pilot than R2-D2.

      With the Smart R2-D2 from Hasbro, kids can imagine sending R2-D2 into the fray of epic intergalactic battles. With app-controlled movement in all directions, Smart R2-D2 is a mobile droid, able to communicate and explore! Use the Hasbro Star Wars Smart R2-D2 app to send Smart R2-D2 on exciting missions and even program him to follow routes — just don't steer him into an asteroid field!

      Star Wars products are produced by Hasbro under license from Lucasfilm Ltd.

      • Communicates and explores
      • Smart R2-D2 plays and dances to music
      • Download the Hasbro Star Wars Smart R2-D2 app for additional gameplay

      It's Cute

      No doubt this R2-D2 is a perfect size. Not too big and not too small. This item would be fantastic for kids, and for Star Wars fanatics. If the animations get a little old, just use it as room decor. The legs of the robot are rather light weight in comparison to its body. The body itself feels more robust. Was it worth the price? I'm not entirely sure. If you're buying primarily for the toy it is and its features, then I would say don't be disappointed if the animations get boring after a while. If you like the robot, regardless of its features, because you're just that big of a star wars fan or you want some house decor, I say go for it! If you are considering to purchase this item for a kid, I say go for it, because if they grow tired of its animations, they can still use their imaginations to think of fun games to play with it. Something to note: the app that works with the robot has a small amount of functions. The arrows that navigate your R2-D2 have some lag, the app itself has some lag. But if you're patient enough, I'm sure it could be quite fun or amaze the kids.
